Writer · Artist · Educator

Farhana Yunnus

Farhana Yunnus is a South African writer, artist and educator whose life moves between Durban and Cape Town. Across the page and the canvas she returns, again and again, to the themes that shape her — faith, motherhood, resilience, and the slow, daily work of healing through words.

Her practice is rooted in Islamic-inspired art and spiritual reflection — a belief that craft can be a form of remembrance, and that beauty made with intention becomes a quiet kind of prayer.

With House of Ayaat, she gathers these threads — writing, art, teaching and mindful making — into a single creative home.

To write to someone you love is to leave a light on for them — for whenever they are ready to find their way home. House of Ayaat

The Book

Dear Waheed

A mother's legacy of love and wisdom in thirty unforgettable letters.

Penguin Random House South Africa

Written through the long stillness of the Covid-19 lockdown, Dear Waheed began as thirty letters from a mother to her son — penned across the nights of Ramadaan and the distance the world had suddenly imposed. What started as a way to hold love close became a luminous meditation on faith, resilience and return: wisdom written for one heart, and offered to many.
