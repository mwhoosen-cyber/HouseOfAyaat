Welcome

Some spaces are built with walls.
Others are built with words.

Ayaat are signs — moments of meaning meant to be noticed, reflected upon, and remembered. House of Ayaat began with a simple belief: that there is meaning to be found in the things we pause long enough to notice.

Inspired by the beauty of the Qur'an and a love of storytelling, this is a space for curious minds, thoughtful hearts, and those who find themselves returning to a sentence long after they have read it — a place where a passage from a book, a verse of reflection, a creative idea, or an ordinary moment can become an invitation to look a little deeper.

Here, words are not simply written; they are explored. Questions are welcomed. Ideas are allowed room to breathe.

Take your time. You are welcome here.

Meet the Founder

Farhana Yunnus

Writer · Educator · Creative Facilitator · Founder of House of Ayaat

I did not set out to build a brand. I simply followed the things that have always felt like home to me: words, stories, learning, creativity, and the quiet spaces where people feel seen.

For as long as I can remember, I have been drawn to stories — not only the ones found in books, but the ones people carry within them: the memories they revisit, the questions they wrestle with, and the moments that quietly shape who they become.

Writing has always been my way of making sense of the world. House of Ayaat came from that place — not a concept for a brand, but a natural extension of how I have always lived with words. As an educator and creative facilitator, I have spent many years encouraging people of all ages to trust their voices and recognise the value of their stories. That work continues here, through writing, workshops, and shared spaces of reflection.

I am the author of Dear Waheed, a collection of letters written to my son during Ramadaan in lockdown, published by Penguin Random House South Africa; and Hana Ayaat: Fragments of Emotions (Breeze Publishing), a reflective collection exploring memory, identity, healing, and the quiet moments that shape our lives.

Today I move between writing, teaching, creating, and holding space for others to do the same. Whether through a workshop, a conversation, or a page of writing, my hope remains simple: to notice what matters, and to give it words before it fades.

Perhaps that is why I write: to remember, to reflect, and to honour the stories we inherit, the journeys we walk, and the people we become along the way. Farhana Yunnus

The Books

Words on the page

Penguin Random House South Africa Dear Waheed

A mother's legacy of love and wisdom in thirty unforgettable letters.

Written through the long stillness of the Covid-19 lockdown, Dear Waheed began as thirty letters from a mother to her son — penned in the early hours after Fajr during Ramadaan, amid the distance and uncertainty the world had suddenly imposed. What started as a way to hold love close became a luminous meditation on faith, resilience and return: wisdom written for one heart, and offered to many.

Letters from the House · The first letter

Dear Heart,

You may not see it yet, but something is unfolding for you. Not always quickly. Not always in the way you imagined. But quietly, beneath what you can measure or control.

There are days when waiting feels like standing still — as if life is happening everywhere else but not here. Yet stillness is not absence. And delay is not denial. What is written for you will not miss you.

So soften your urgency. Loosen your grip on the outcome. Breathe in the space you are standing in. You are not forgotten in this moment — you are being held within it.

There is a wisdom in timing that the heart learns slowly. And sometimes, what we call “not yet” is simply life being careful with us. Even now, something is taking shape. Even now, you are becoming someone who can receive it.

With patience,
The One who writes your story already knows its ending.

Whispers of Hope · The first whisper

For those who are waiting,

For those whose prayers feel heavy. For those who have not given up hope, even when hope feels fragile.

“Perhaps the tears in your prayers are not signs of breaking, but signs of becoming. Seeds are watered before they bloom.”

May these words keep you company on the journey between prayer and answer.

From the Heritage archive

Some things deserve to be written down.

Not because they are extraordinary, but because they are yours. The way your grandmother folded dough with practiced hands. The phrase your father repeated whenever life became difficult. The recipe stained with fingerprints and memory. The story told so many times that everyone knows the ending.

These are the threads that hold a family together. One day, someone may search for where they came from and find the answer in the words you chose to preserve.

Write it down — the ordinary moments are often the ones that become heirlooms.
